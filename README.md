      <div class="card">
        <h2>Study Purpose</h2>
        <p>
          This study compares combinations of fertility indicators—<strong>cervical mucus</strong>, <strong>urinary LH</strong>,
          <strong>urinary PDG</strong>, and <strong>basal body temperature (BBT)</strong>—to learn which method most accurately
          identifies ovulation and estimates luteal phase length. (Premom® app-based tracking.)
        </p>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <h2>Who Can Participate</h2>
        <ul>
          <li>Age <strong>16–45</strong></li>
          <li>Menstrual cycles <strong>35 days or fewer</strong></li>
          <li>Off hormonal contraception for at least <strong>3 months</strong></li>
          <li>Already using the <strong>Premom® App</strong> to track cycles</li>
          <li>Live within <strong>50 km of Ottawa, Canada</strong></li>
          <li>Not currently pregnant or breastfeeding</li>
        </ul>
        <p class="small">
          A research assistant will confirm eligibility after you express interest.
        </p>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <h2>What Participation Involves</h2>
        <ul>
          <li>Track fertility signs for <strong>3 menstrual cycles</strong> (about 3–4 months).</li>
          <li>Record daily information in the Premom® app.</li>
          <li>Observe and record cervical mucus (simple scoring system).</li>
          <li>Use urine test strips for <strong>LH</strong> and <strong>PDG</strong> (provided at no cost).</li>
          <li>Measure <strong>BBT</strong> each morning with a thermometer (provided at no cost).</li>
          <li>Upload photos of LH and PDG strips to the app.</li>
          <li>Upload tracking information to the study’s secure OneDrive portal.</li>
          <li>Complete a short questionnaire at the end of the study.</li>
        </ul>

        <div class="note small">
          You do not need to come to the clinic — the study is completed entirely from home.
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <h2>Time Commitment</h2>
        <p>
          Daily tracking takes about <strong>5–10 minutes/day</strong>. Over 3–4 months, the total time commitment is
          approximately <strong>15–16 hours</strong>, including brief check-ins.
        </p>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <h2>Risks and Benefits</h2>
        <p>
          This is a <strong>low-risk</strong> study. Testing is non-invasive (urine testing, temperature measurement, and mucus observation).
          Some people may experience mild inconvenience (time commitment) or mild emotional discomfort when reviewing fertility results.
        </p>
        <p>
          You may gain a better understanding of your cycle patterns. You will receive test supplies at no cost.
          While there may be no direct personal benefit, your participation helps improve fertility tracking methods.
        </p>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <h2>Privacy</h2>
        <p>
          Your information will be <strong>coded</strong> (e.g., ID01) to protect your identity. Identifying information is stored separately,
          securely, and only accessible to the research team. Data are stored securely in the Bruyère-hosted OneDrive portal.
          Only aggregate results will be reported.
        </p>
      </div>
